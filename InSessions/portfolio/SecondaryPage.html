Experiences

During one of my volunteering experiences, I had the opportunity to assist in packing marathon bags filled with a variety of items, including medals, lipstick, chocolate wafer bars, nuts, and mini Persil detergent, a laundry essential. The process was both organized and dynamic. My role primarily involved unpacking heavy packs of medals and replacing empty bags for the other volunteers who were seated at tables and diligently loading the bags. This teamwork-driven activity highlighted the importance of coordination and efficiency, as we worked together to prepare the marathon essentials for participants. The experience was rewarding, as it combined physical effort, attention to detail, and camaraderie.

I had the privilege of volunteering with an NGO initiative called "Iftar in a Box," aimed at packing and donating food supplies to families during Ramadan. Each type of produce—potatoes, tomatoes, cucumbers, zucchini, lettuce, eggplant, lemons, and cabbage—was weighed to specific quantities (e.g., 2 kg of lemons, 1.5 kg of cucumbers) and packed into separate bags. These were then consolidated into large bags weighing about 15 kg each. We packed countless bags, and the workspace became increasingly cramped with supplies. Despite the challenges, the teamwork and purpose kept us motivated, knowing our efforts would bring relief and joy to many families.

SMEX, a non-profit organization advocating for digital rights in the West Asia and North Africa region, hosted a 4-hour session on digital security at Hotel Ramada. SMEX focuses on promoting safe and responsible digital engagement by providing resources, conducting research, and supporting freedom of expression online​. During the session, we covered various critical aspects of online safety. Topics included understanding viruses and Trojan applications, creating strong passwords and utilizing password managers, and the importance of VPNs—how they work and which ones are the safest. We also learned to identify phishing links, spot fake emails, and take precautions when opening messages from unknown senders or connecting to public Wi-Fi. The session provided practical tools for protecting ourselves in an increasingly digital world.

I had the opportunity to attend a session hosted by Al Jazeera journalist Elia Ghorbiah, focusing on storytelling and mobile journalism. During the session, we explored what makes stories compelling, avoiding common pitfalls, and incorporating emotions appropriately. Elia also demonstrated mobile journalism tools, from cameras to microphones and apps, tailoring content for platforms like YouTube and TikTok based on their unique audiences. This hands-on experience provided valuable insights into creating engaging, audience-specific narratives using modern technology.
